# ADR-NNN: Short decision title

- **Status:** Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by ADR-XXX

## Context

What is going on? What problem or pressure is forcing us to decide now? Keep it to the facts a newcomer would need to understand the choice — no preamble, no history lesson.

## Decision

What we are doing. State it plainly, as one or two sentences. Then expand only if the sentence isn't enough to act on.

## Consequences

What changes because of this decision — both the wins and the costs we're accepting. Include things that get harder, not just things that get easier.

- **Positive:** ...
- **Negative:** ...
- **Neutral / follow-up:** ...

## Alternatives considered

At least two, each with one or two sentences on why we rejected it. Reviewers will scrutinise this section harder than the Decision section — if it's thin, the decision probably wasn't stress-tested.

- **Alternative A** — why rejected.
- **Alternative B** — why rejected.
